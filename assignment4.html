Challenge 5: Accessibility Concern

As I was doing some research on what I should include to make my page more accessible, I did my best to apply what I could to each section to follow said advice. I stated by placing a language marker after the html tab to list what language the page is in. From there I got to work tyring to make my Checkerboard more accessible by first adding a caption at the top of the table describing it as a stalemate. In addition, I gave each box an id with the number and letter placement similar to an actual checkerboard. On the same ids, if the box had a chesspiece I added it to the description. I did something similar to the ids for the chessboard on the form. I added some id attributes to the options, although they could be more descriptive some do demonstrate what the questions are asking. Please ignore my Challenge 4 in terms of accessibility for now since it is still a work in progress. I didn't use any images so I didn't need to add any alt tags, honestly my biggest concern was the accessibility issues in a table, which is what I used for the chessboard. Tables frequently have accessiblity issues due to their nature and form. Visually, they can display informatino cohesively, but outside of that the code displays in a detremental way for accessiblity. Hopefully I made the chessboard a little more accessible through my previously mentioned changes, as that was certianly my biggest concern.
